    Berkeley Food Pantry
    (All Ages)
    Our volunteer positions require a six-month commitment. Most of our volunteer shifts are on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday and are 3 hours long. The shifts are staggered between 12:30 and 4:30 pm. We also use volunteers to pick up food donations on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays before the pantry opens at 2pm. We have occasional need for Saturday volunteers between 9:30am and 12:30pm. These positions are one-time volunteer opportunities and do not require a six-month commitment.
    Address: 1600 Sacramento Street Berkeley, CA 94702
    To apply for an open volunteer position,email us volunteer@berkeleyfoodpantry.org or call (510) 525-2280

    The Lawrence Hall Of Science
    (Grades 9+)
    The Lawrence Hall of Science has a fun and dynamic program for teens. All teens enter the program in May, volunteering on the exhibit floor for the summer or throughout the year. This is a great way for teens to fulfill community service requirements, gain job skills, and learn about communicating science to the public. Volunteering here will give teens great experience in public speaking, education, customer service, and general job skills. Volunteers will work with Hall staff, UC Berkeley college interns, and other teens from all around the Bay Area. Able to attend the Teen Orientation on April 7, 2018
    • Entering 9th grade or above
    • Able to work an average of one four-hour shift per week (June–August)
    • Able to show interest in topics related to science, technology, engineering, or mathematics
    • Committed to working with our visitors, some of whom are families with children
    • Volunteer work will include:
        --Facilitating hands-on engineering activities in Design Quest, our featured experience, or in the Ingenuity Lab, an engineering design space, or assisting our staff in supporting summer camp-related activities.
        --Assisting staff in delivery of Summer Science Fun Days, our themed summer activities.
        --Providing enthusiastic, quality customer service.

    Ecology Center
    (Ages 14-22)
    The Ecology Center’s Youth Environmental Academy (YEA) is a green leadership development program for young people ages 14-22. YEA provides structured, paid internships to youth at our headquarters in West Berkeley. Through programming based on the nationally acclaimed Roots of Success curriculum, youth will emerge with content knowledge across the following areas:
    1) Health, Food Systems, Food Justice, and Sustainable Agriculture,
    2) Alternative Energy,
    3) Climate Change,
    4) Water Conservation, and
    5) Waste Management and Recycling.
    Through guided, hands-on projects led by professionals, youth will also gain job skills that will prepare them for green careers. YEA participants “earn and learn” while moving to positions of greater responsibility and leadership – from Youth Scholars to Youth Interns to Youth Program Assistants. The ultimate outcome is a new generation of environmentally conscious community leaders! For more information, Click Here
